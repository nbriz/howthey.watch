References and Resources

For starters...

  • Surya Mattu and Aaron Sankin at the Markup have a great write-up explaining how they built Blacklight, their real-time privacy inspector I referenced in the "Tracking the Trackers" section of the Immediate Personal Solutions page. In my opinion it also happens to be the best primer on online tracking today, it's accessible, concise and well researched.
  • For a more academic take, this Princeton paper Online Tracking: A 1-million-site Measurement and Analysis by Steven Englehardt and Arvind Narayanan is also great.


In the "Alternatives to Surveillance Platforms" section of the Immediate Personal Solutions page of this site I discuss some of my personal privacy conscious app/platform choices, but I also mention that there is no one-size-fits-all solution to online privacy, we've all got different needs and challenges. So here are a few other great guides for your consideration...

  • A DIY Guide to Feminist Cybersecurity by Noah Kelley
  • Surveillance Self-Defense by the EFF
  • Security in a Box by Tactical Technology Collective and Front Line Defenders


Learn More

Here's a list of references if you want to learn more about online tracking, the business of surveillance capitalism and the various issues associated with it. As I've mentioned in the other pages, this is by no means an authoritative list, this is just a small curated collection from my own personal research on the subject:

  • (book) You Are Not A Gadget (2010) and Who Owns The Future (2012) by Jaron Lanier
  • (book) Data and Goliath: The Hidden Battles to Collect Your Data and Control Your World (2015) by Bruce Schneier
  • (book) Weapons of Math Destruction: How big data increases inequality and threatens democracy (2016) by Cathy O'Neil
  • (book) The Age of Surveillance Capitalism: The Fight for a Human Future at the New Frontier of Power (2019) by Shoshana Zuboff
  • (website) Me and My Shadow by Tactical Technology Collective
  • (hypermedia documentary) Do Not Track (2015) by Brett Gaylor
  • (documentary) Coded Bias (2020) by Shalini Kantayya
  • (documentary) The Social Dilemma (2020) by Jeff Orlowski, Vickie Curtis and Davis Coombe
